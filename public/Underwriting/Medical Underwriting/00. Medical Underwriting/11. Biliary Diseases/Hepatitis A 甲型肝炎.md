Is an inflammation of the liver caused by the hepatitis A virus (HAV). The virus is primarily spread when an uninfected (and unvaccinated) person ingests food or water that is contaminated with the faeces of an infected person.

Hepatitis A is an acute or short-term infection, which means people usually get better without treatment after a few weeks. No specific treatment exists for hepatitis A. Your body will clear the hepatitis A virus on its own. In most cases of hepatitis A, the liver heals within six months with no lasting damage.

Symptoms may last for several weeks, but most people fully recover. Recovering from a hepatitis A infection or being vaccinated will provide lifelong immunity against hepatitis A. This does not provide immunity or protection against hepatitis B or hepatitis C.

## FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

> 1)  Actual & date diagnosis ?
> 2)  Any investigation tests such endoscopy,  blood & urine tests etc… ? If yes, please state the dates & results.
> 3)  On medication / treatment ? If yes, what type & period of treatment.
> 4)  Still on medical follow up ? If yes, how frequent (weekly / monthly etc) ?
