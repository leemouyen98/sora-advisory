#Medical_Underwriting/Biliary_Diseases

The presence of stones in the gall bladder.  

Gallstones may form if bile contains too much cholesterol, too much bilirubin, or not enough bile salts. Gallstones can block the tubes (ducts) through which bile flows from your gallbladder or liver to your small intestine. Severe pain, jaundice and bile duct infection can result.

Surgical removal of the gallbladder or gallstones. Around 80 per cent of people with gallstone symptoms will need surgery. Surgeons may remove your entire gallbladder (cholecystectomy), or just the stones from bile ducts.

## FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

> 1)  Actual & date diagnosis ?
> 2)  Any investigation tests such endoscopy,  blood & urine tests etc… ? If yes, please state the dates & results.
> 3)  On medication / treatment ? If yes, what type & period of treatment.
> 4)  Still on medical follow up ? If yes, how frequent (weekly / monthly etc) ?

## COMMON REQUIREMENTS

> 1)  From client / doctor
> a)  Any relevant reports such as blood (Liver function Test) / urine / scan test ?
>
> 2)  Medical attendant report (MAR) / Attending physician statement (APS)
