# HOARSENSES
# 声音嘶哑

The most common causes of chronic hoarseness are: Overusing your voice, which is most common in individuals who use their voices professionally (performing artists, teachers, salespersons and speakers) Persistent bacterial or viral infection. Polyps, cysts or nodules on your vocal cords.

# COUGH
# 咳嗽

Is a reflex action to clear your airways of mucus and irritants such as dust or smoke. It’s rarely a sign of anything serious. Most coughs clear up within 3 weeks and don’t require any treatment. A dry cough means it’s tickly and doesn’t produce any phlegm (thick mucus).

Occasional coughing is normal as it helps clear your throat and airway of germs, mucus and dust. A cough that doesn’t go away or comes with other symptoms like shortness of breath, mucus production or bloody phlegm could be the sign of a more serious medical problem.

> 5) Fully recovered ?
> 5) 是否已完全康复？
