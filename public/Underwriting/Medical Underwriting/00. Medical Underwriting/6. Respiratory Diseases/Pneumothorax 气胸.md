#Medical_Underwriting/Respiratory_Diseases

Air in the pleural cavity. Any breach of the lung surface or chest wall allows air to enter the pleural cavity, causing the lung to collapse.

It may result from chest trauma, excess pressure on the lungs or a lung disease, such as chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), asthma, cystic fibrosis, tuberculosis or whooping cough.

![[ Pneumothorax 01.png]]

## FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

> 1) Actual & date diagnosis ?
> 2) Any investigation tests such chest X-ray, lung function test, blood & urine tests etc.. ? If yes, please state the dates & results.
> 3) On medication / treatment ? If yes, what type & period of treatment.
> 4) Still on medical follow up ? If yes, how frequent (weekly / monthly etc) ?
> 5) Fully recovered ?

## COMMON REQUIREMENTS

> 1) From client / doctor
> a) Any relevant reports such as blood / urine / chest X-ray, scan tests ?
> b) Surgery report if performed.
>
> 2) Medical attendant report (MAR) / Attending physician statement (APS)

## EXAMPLE OF DISCLOSURE

> Diagnosed to have pneumothorax due to accident on dd/mm/yy by Dr xxx of yy Hospital, admitted for surgery, well treated & no complication noted.
> Discharged well. Scanning, blood & urine test done on dd/mm/yy is attached.
> Fully recovered since.

## POSSIBILITIES OF UNDERWRITING OUTCOMES

> Life / TPD / CI / Medical insurance:
> – Postpone till full recovery with normal lung function
