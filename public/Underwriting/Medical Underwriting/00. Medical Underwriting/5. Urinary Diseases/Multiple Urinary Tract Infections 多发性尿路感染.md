#Medical_Underwriting/Urinary_Diseases

A urinary tract infection (UTI) is an infection of the urinary system.

Multiple/recurrent UTI are common in women, including healthy women with normal genitourinary anatomy. Recurrent UTI is typically defined as three or more UTIs within 12 months, or two or more occurrences within six months.

![[Multiple Urinary Tract Infections 01.png]]

## FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

> 1) Actual & date diagnosis ?
> 2) Any investigation tests such scanning, blood & urine tests etc... ? If yes, please state the dates & results.
> 3) On medication / treatment ? If yes, what type & period of treatment.
> 4) Still on medical follow up ? If yes, how frequent (weekly / monthly etc) ?
> 5) Fully recovered ?

## COMMON REQUIREMENTS

> 1) From client / doctor
> a) Any relevant reports such as blood / urine / scan test ?
>
> 2) Medical Examination / Medical attendant report (MAR) / Attending physician statement (APS) from attending doctor

## EXAMPLE OF DISCLOSURE

> Diagnosed to have urinary tract infection on dd/mm/yy. Seen by Dr xxx of yy Hospital.
> On short course of medication, fully recovered since.
> Blood & urine test were done on dd/mm/yy. Reports are attached here.

## POSSIBILITIES OF UNDERWRITING OUTCOMES

> Life / TPD / CI:
> – Standard case provided well treated & no complications
>
> Medical insurance:
> – KIV decline if still have recurrent infections
