#Medical_Underwriting/Urinary_Diseases

A condition in which there is great loss of protein in the urine, reduced levels of albumin in the blood and generalized swelling of the tissues due to oedema. It can be caused by a variety of disorders, most usually glomerulonephritis.

Nephrotic syndrome is a group of symptoms that show that your kidneys are not working as well as they should. While the prognosis is usually quite good, nephrotic syndrome can become severe and even potentially life- threatening, if left untreated. The degree of severity depends on the underlying cause.

## FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

> 1) Actual & date diagnosis ?
> 2) Any investigation tests such scanning, blood (renal function test) & urine FEME tests etc... ? If yes, please state the dates & results.
> 3) On medication / treatment ? If yes, what type & period of treatment.
> 4) Still on medical follow up ? If yes, how frequent (weekly / monthly etc) ?
> 5) Fully recovered ?

## COMMON REQUIREMENTS

> 1) From client / doctor
> a) Any relevant reports such as blood (renal function test) / urine FEME / Ultrasound scan test ?
> 
> 2) Medical Examination & Medical attendant report (MAR) / Attending physician statement (APS) from attending doctor

## EXAMPLE OF DISCLOSURE

> Diagnosed to have nephrotis syndrome on dd/mm/yy. Seen by Dr xxx of yy Hospital.
> Ultrasound, blood & urine test were done on dd/mm/yy. Reports are attached here.
> On regular medical follow up

## POSSIBILITIES OF UNDERWRITING OUTCOMES

> Life / TPD / CI / Medical insurance:
> – Present & frequent recurrence – Decline case
> – Can be standard / substandard if the episode more than 2 years with full recovery, normal ultrasound of kidney
> – Can be standard if the episode more than 5 years
