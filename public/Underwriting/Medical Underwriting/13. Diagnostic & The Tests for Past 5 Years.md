#Medical_Underwriting/Diagnostic_Tests_For_Past_5_Years 

In the PAST FIVE YEARS, have you had any tests done such as angiogram, X-ray, ultrasound, CT scan, PET scan, MRI, biopsy, echocardiogram  (ECG), blood, or urine test, mammogram, pap smear or other diagnostic test or are you currently awaiting the results of any test or advised to undergo or repeat  any test ?

If yes, please state type, reason, date of test done and results of test (copy to be submitted if available).

# Angiogram 冠状动脉造影

Is a procedure in which a very thin catheter, or tube, is threaded through an artery from the groin, neck, or arm to the coronary arteries around the heart. The doctor uses this catheter to put a contrast dye, usually containing iodine, and x ray pictures to detect blockages in the coronary arteries that are caused by plaque buildup.

# X-Ray X射线

Is a quick, painless test that produces images of the structures inside your body, particularly your bones. X-ray beams pass through your body, and they are absorbed in different amounts depending on the density of the material they pass through.

# Ultrasound 超声波

Is an imaging test that uses sound waves to create a picture (sonogram) of organs, tissues, and other structures inside the body. Unlike x-rays, ultrasounds don’t use any radiation.

Is a medical imaging technique used in radiology to get detailed images of the body noninvasively for diagnostic purposes.

# Positron Emission Tomography (PET) Scan 正电子发射断层扫描

Is a functional imaging technique that uses radioactive substances known as radiotracers to visualize and measure changes in metabolic processes, and in other physiological activities including blood flow, regional chemical composition, and absorption.

# Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) 核磁共振

Is a type of scan that uses strong magnetic fields and radio waves to produce detailed images of the inside of the body. An MRI scanner is a large tube that contains powerful magnets.

# Blood Test 血液测试

Blood Tests is a scientific examination of a sample of blood, typically for the diagnosis of illness or for the detection and measurement of drugs or other substances.

# Urine Test 尿液测试

Urine Tests is a simple test that looks at a small sample of your urine. It can help find problems that need treatment, including infections or kidney problems. It can also help find serious diseases in the early stages, like kidney disease, diabetes, or liver disease.

![[Urine Test 01.png]]

# Mammogram 乳房X光检查

Is the process of using low-energy X-rays to examine the human breast for diagnosis and screening. The goal of mammography is the early detection of breast cancer, typically through detection of characteristic masses or microcalcifications.

# Pap (Papanicolaou) Smear 巴氏涂片

Is a procedure to test for cervical cancer in women. A Pap smear involves collecting cells from your cervix. Detecting cervical cancer early with a Pap smear gives you a greater chance at a cure.

# Cardiac Catheterisation 心导管检查

Is a procedure used to diagnose and treat certain cardiovascular conditions. During cardiac catheterisation, a long thin tube called a catheter is inserted in an artery or vein in your groin, neck or arm and threaded through your blood vessels to your heart.

# Electro Encephalography (EEG) 脑电图

Is a test that detects electrical activity in your brain using small, metal discs (electrodes) attached to your scalp. Your brain cells communicate via electrical impulses and are active all the time, even when you’re asleep. This activity shows up as wavy lines on an EEG recording.

![[Electro Encephalography 01.png]]

# Lumbar Puncture 腰椎穿刺

May be done to diagnose or treat a condition. The doctor inserts a hollow needle into the space surrounding the spinal column (subarachnoid space) in the lower back to withdraw some cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) or inject medicine.

# Nerve Conduction Studies (NCS) 神经传导研究

Is a medical diagnostic test to measures how fast an electrical impulse moves through the nerve. NCS can identify nerve damage. During the test, nerve is stimulated, usually with electrode patches attached to the skin.

![[Nerve Conduction Studies 01.png]]

# Scope 内窥镜

A scope procedure involves placing a special flexible tube with a camera lens at the tip (endoscope) into part of the body. This allows the insides of certain organs to be viewed on a monitor screen. The most common scope procedures are colonoscopy and upper endoscopy (also called EGD).

An upper endoscopy usually takes 20 to 30 minutes to complete. When the procedure is over, the doctor will gently remove the endoscope. Then you will go to a recovery room.

![[Scope 01.png]]

# Sleep Study / Polysomnography 睡眠研究/多导睡眠图

Is a test used to diagnose sleep disorders. Polysomnography records your brain waves, the oxygen level in your blood, and your heart rate and breathing during sleep. It also measures eye and leg movements.

You may need this test if you have symptoms of a sleep disorder, such as: Loud snoring during sleep. Waking up from sleep and gasping for breath. Trouble falling and/or staying asleep.
